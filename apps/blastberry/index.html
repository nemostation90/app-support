Help & Support

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about playing Blastberry.

Aim the cannon at the bottom of the screen and tap to fire a fruit at the cluster above. Three or more fruits of the same color in a row pop and explode. Clear the level's quota of fruits before you run out of shots to win the level.

Each world introduces a new sub-genre: Worlds 1–3 are classic cluster shooting, World 4 is a Zuma-style chain along a curved path, and World 5 is Cascade Tracks with up to three streams flowing at once.

Blastberry ships six power-ups: Rainbow Fruit, Bomb Fruit, +5 Bonus Shots, Bird Repellent, Time Freeze, and Hint. Each one helps you in a tight spot. You can earn them through play, watch a rewarded ad to claim one, or buy them in the Shop with coins.

Tap a power-up icon in the gameplay tray to activate it. Some are one-tap (Bomb), others change how you aim or shoot for the rest of the level.

Premium Unlock is a one-time purchase ($7.99 — no subscription). It includes:

  • Access to all currently shipped worlds.
  • No ads (interstitial ads are removed; rewarded ads remain optional).
  • 2× coin earn rate on every level.
  • A daily check-in bonus that grants coins or a power-up each day you play.

Premium auto-extends as new worlds ship — there is no extra charge for Worlds 6 and 7 when they launch.

Open Settings → Restore Purchases inside Blastberry while signed in to the same Apple ID you used originally. Apple's StoreKit will re-grant Premium Unlock, Remove Ads, and the Welcome Pack automatically.

Note: Coin packs and gem packs are consumable items — they cannot be restored because they are spent on the device when you use them. Only entitlements (Premium, Remove Ads) restore.

Game progress (levels completed, stars, coins, owned power-ups) is stored locally on your device. If you have iCloud Backup enabled in iOS Settings, your progress is preserved when you restore a device from backup.

If you reinstalled the app without an iCloud backup, the on-device save was deleted with the app. Restore Purchases will re-grant any items you bought, but level progress cannot be recovered. We are working on cross-device cloud save in a future update.

Buy Remove Ads in the Shop ($1.99, one-time) — this removes interstitial ads forever. Rewarded ads are always optional and you can ignore them. Premium Unlock includes Remove Ads automatically.

You can also opt out of personalized ads (without paying) by going to iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → Blastberry and turning the toggle off. You will still see ads, but they will be contextual rather than targeted.

Blastberry rotates 11 fruit packs across the calendar year — Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter, plus festivals: New Year, Valentine, Easter, Pride, Halloween, Thanksgiving, and Christmas. Each pack swaps in season-themed fruit sprites so the game feels fresh year-round.

The active theme follows the calendar by default, but you can override it manually in Settings → Themes → Active theme.

Worlds unlock progressively as you complete the levels in the prior world. World 1 is open from the start; later worlds open as you collect stars on the levels before them.

If you would rather skip the wait, Premium Unlock grants instant access to every currently shipped world.

Email pals_green.1m@icloud.com with: a short description, the level / world / mode where it happened, and (if you can) a screenshot or screen recording. We read every message.

Include your iOS version and device model if the bug is visual — that helps a lot. Bug reports are triaged within 24 hours.

Privacy Policy

We do not run user accounts and we do not sell your data. Here is exactly what Blastberry collects and how it uses it.

Effective date: 2026-05-15

TL;DR

  • We do not ask for your name, email, address, phone number, or any account credentials. The app has no login.
  • The app stores your progress only on your device.
  • If you tap "Allow Tracking" on Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt, the advertising SDK may use Apple's IDFA to show you more relevant ads. If you decline, no tracking identifier is shared and you see contextual (non-personalized) ads.
  • Purchases go through Apple's StoreKit. We see only the receipt Apple sends us; we never see your credit card or Apple ID.
  • Uninstalling the app removes all on-device data. There is no server-side copy to delete.

1. What we collect — from you, on this device

  • Game progress: levels completed, stars earned, coins, gems, cosmetic ownership, and settings (audio, haptics, notifications). Stored locally using Apple's standard storage (SwiftData and UserDefaults). Not uploaded to any server we control.
  • Purchase history (StoreKit receipts): when you buy a product such as Remove Ads, the Welcome Pack, the Premium Unlock, or a coin or gem pack, Apple sends the app a receipt that proves the purchase. We use that receipt to grant your entitlement and to support Restore Purchases. The receipt does not contain your name, email, or payment details.

2. From third parties — only when you consent

  • Advertising identifier (IDFA), only if you allow tracking: on first launch (or when iOS requires re-prompting) you see Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt. If you tap "Allow", the advertising SDK is permitted to read Apple's IDFA so ads can be personalized. If you tap "Ask App Not to Track", no IDFA is shared and you see contextual ads instead.
  • Approximate device information for ads: the advertising SDK receives standard ad-request metadata — iOS version, device model class, language, country, and a coarse network type. This is what every ad-supported app sends; ad networks use it to choose which ad to serve. It is not combined with your personal identity by us.

3. From Apple — for crash reports

If you have crash reporting enabled in your iOS Settings, Apple may send us aggregated, anonymized crash reports via App Store Connect. These reports contain a stack trace and a device model but no information that identifies you personally. You can disable this at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements.

4. SDKs and third parties

Blastberry uses a small number of third-party software components ("SDKs"). Each one is listed below with its purpose and the data it sees.

  • Apple StoreKit (built into iOS) — processes in-app purchases. See Apple's Privacy Policy.
  • Google AdMob (advertising mediation, used only after you allow tracking, or in non-personalized mode if you don't) — coordinates ad requests across networks. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google User Messaging Platform — shows the EU consent form before any ad requests in EEA / UK regions. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Apple GameKit / Game Center (optional — only if you opt into leaderboards) — when you submit a score, your Game Center alias is shown alongside it on the leaderboard. We do not store the alias outside Game Center.

We do not use Facebook SDK, AppLovin MAX, Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any social-login SDK.

5. How we share data

We do not sell your data. We do not share data with brokers, social networks, or analytics vendors.

The only data flow off your device is:

  1. Apple's StoreKit receives your purchase request (this is how every paid iOS app works).
  2. Ad SDKs receive an ad request when an ad is about to be shown. With tracking allowed, the request may include your IDFA. Without tracking allowed, the request does not include IDFA; you see contextual ads.

That is the complete list.

6. Data retention

  • On-device data lives until you delete the app. Reinstalling the app starts fresh; there is no cloud backup of progress unless you have iCloud Backup of your device enabled (in which case Apple, not us, holds the backup).
  • StoreKit purchase records are retained by Apple per Apple's policy so Restore Purchases continues to work.
  • Ad requests do not produce a retained record on our side. Each ad network keeps its own logs per its own policy linked above.

7. Your rights

Because we hold no personal data about you on any server we operate, there is no account to delete or export from us. You can exercise the following controls directly:

  • Delete all data: uninstall Blastberry. This permanently removes every record this app stored on your device.
  • Revoke tracking consent: iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → Blastberry → toggle off. Or use the in-app "Re-show consent form" / "Reset app tracking preference" buttons in Settings.
  • Restore Purchases: Settings → Restore Purchases. Uses Apple's existing receipt; no new data is collected.
  • EU / UK users (GDPR, UK-GDPR): the legal basis for processing your ad data is your consent, freely given via the ATT prompt and the EU consent form, and you may withdraw it at any time using the controls above. There is no profile we hold to export or correct.
  • California users (CCPA / CPRA): we do not sell personal information. You have the right to opt out of any future sale; we will publish an opt-out link if that ever changes.

8. Children's privacy

Blastberry is age-rated 9+ in the App Store. It is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. The app has no chat, no name field, no photo upload, and no profile. If you believe a child under 13 has somehow provided personal information to us, contact pals_green.1m@icloud.com and we will delete it.

9. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the effective date at the top and post the updated version here. Material changes (for example, adding a new SDK or a new data-sharing partner) will be highlighted in the app the next time you launch it.

10. Contact
